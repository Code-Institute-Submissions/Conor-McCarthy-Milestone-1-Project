Q&A with Conor

Can mindfullness help you find more meaning in your day to day life?

Mindfullness can help you appreciate the present moment in life. You'll soon discover that whatever you want to accomplish in life , the happiness brought by it is fleeting and you'll never stay happy. This is the backbones of the practice.

Can mindfullness benefit your career?

You'll start to notice that thoughts and emotions are fleeting, they'll arise and pass away. This can be hugely beneficial for calming yourself down in the scenario where a co-worker might get on your nerves and you have the inner feeling of wanting to scream at them.

How can you make meditation a habit?

The key to this is to start small and dont miss a day. You should start with our 5 minute guided meditation sessions and do that for 60 days in a row, this is how long it takes before it's fully built in habit in your brain. After that, it'll be painful to miss a session.

What are some ways you can remain mindful outside of sitting meditation?

For the next 30 seconds, try and pay attention to the breath without any mind wandering. You will fail. Your inability to do this should interest you. Only further training of the mind can you better accomplish this goal.

Aren't we our thoughts and feelings? How can observing them be helpful?

No, they're merely a product of your mind that you had no say in them appearing. They're no more substancial than the words you're reading on this screen.The lack of identification of thoughts and emotions actually reduces the negative effects of them drastically, the knowing that they're not "yours".

How long and often should I meditate for?

Start for 5 minutes and the key is to never miss a single day. The way to build ontop of this is to add 30 seconds to a minute after every sit after its become an established habit (after 60 days) and that 5 minutes can increase to 35 in 2 months or less!

I am a Christian who believes in God,wouldn't learning meditation interfere with my beliefs?

There is nothing strictly religious about meditation, despite common belief there is actaully very little religious connotations to meditation.

Can meditation be a detriment to someone with Ptsd?

It's known that for approximately 1% of the population that meditation can bring out previous tramas during a sit. If this is the case, I recommend that you stop sitting immediately. If this happens in any of your future sits, unfortunately you might need to give up the practice until your ptsd subsides.

What other lifestyle choices go well together with meditation?

The overall feelings of wellbeing can make meditation that much easier. I recommend you remian physically active aswell as eating healthy to have positive crossover effect with meditation.
